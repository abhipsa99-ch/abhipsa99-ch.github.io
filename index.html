About Me

Hello! I am Abhipsa Chakraborty, a Renewable Energy Engineer specialized in Wind Energy and a Biotechnology graduate from India. My journey has been driven by curiosity, continuous learning, and a desire to contribute to technologies that create a cleaner and more sustainable future.

I was born and raised in India, where I completed my schooling. From an early age I enjoyed science and mathematics and was fascinated by understanding how things work. School helped me develop discipline, problem-solving skills, and a strong academic foundation that inspired me to pursue engineering and scientific research.

I completed my Bachelor of Technology in Biotechnology, where I gained knowledge in molecular biology, microbiology, laboratory techniques, bioprocess engineering, and analytical methods. During this period I also completed an internship at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kharagpur, working on biodiesel production from Jatropha plants, which strengthened my research skills and interest in sustainable technologies.

My growing interest in sustainability motivated me to pursue the European Master in Renewable Energy (EUREC), specializing in Wind Energy at the National Technical University of Athens (NTUA), Greece. Studying in Europe allowed me to work with students from different countries while gaining practical experience in renewable energy systems, wind turbine aerodynamics, wind resource assessment, numerical modelling, and engineering design.

Throughout my academic journey I have worked on projects involving wind turbine construction, wind tunnel testing, Cp–λ performance evaluation, renewable energy analysis, and laboratory research. These experiences strengthened my analytical thinking, technical problem-solving abilities, and teamwork skills.

Apart from academics, I enjoy learning new technologies, travelling, exploring different cultures, meeting people from around the world, and continuously improving myself both personally and professionally. I believe every experience contributes to growth and broadens my perspective.

My long-term goal is to contribute to innovative renewable energy projects that support the global transition towards clean energy. I am particularly interested in wind energy, renewable power generation, engineering analysis, research, and sustainable technologies that create real-world impact.
